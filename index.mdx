Showkase automatically generates a UI browser for your Jetpack Compose design system. With minimal setup, every component, color, and typography style in your codebase becomes searchable and previewable — including auto-generated permutations for dark mode, RTL, and font scaling.

## Why Showkase?

When building component-based UIs with Jetpack Compose, codebases quickly accumulate hundreds of components that become hard to find and visualize. Showkase solves this by turning your annotations into a living, searchable UI browser — with zero manual maintenance.

## How it works

<Steps>
  <Step title="Add dependencies">
    Add the Showkase runtime, annotation, and processor to your `build.gradle`.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Annotate your UI elements">
    Use `@ShowkaseComposable`, `@ShowkaseColor`, and `@ShowkaseTypography` — or let existing `@Preview` annotations do the work.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Define a root module">
    Create a class implementing `ShowkaseRootModule` and annotate it with `@ShowkaseRoot`.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Launch the browser">
    Call `Showkase.getBrowserIntent(context)` to open the auto-generated UI browser anywhere in your app.
  </Step>
</Steps>
